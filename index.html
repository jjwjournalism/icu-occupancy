COVID hospitalizations are down, but some New York City ICUs are still crowded

By: Jaclyn Jeffrey-Wilensky

Even as COVID hospitalizations and deaths come down from their post-holiday spike, many New York City intensive care units remain close to capacity, limiting their ability to treat seriously ill COVID patients.

At Jamaica Hospital Medical Center, NYC Health + Hospitals/Woodhull, New York Community Hospital, and Staten Island University Hospital, fewer than five percent of ICU beds were available last week, according to data released by the Department of Health and Human Services. COVID patients occupy at least half of these hospitals’ ICU beds.

Elsewhere in the city, ICUs have ample room for seriously ill patients. Mount Sinai Hospital, Lenox Hill Hospital, Richmond University Medical Center, and New York-Presbyterian Queens Hospital all have at least half their ICU beds open.

Citywide, about 77 percent of ICU beds were occupied last week, above the 70 percent threshold originally required to begin phased reopening in New York State. Total ICU capacity has hovered around 75 percent since early January.

It’s not uncommon for ICUs to operate with more full beds than empty ones. Studies conducted before the onset of the pandemic suggest that American ICUs average between 65 and 70 percent occupancy. But as they approach full capacity, hospitals struggle to treat the most critically ill patients, including those with severe COVID-19.
